Introduction

Smallpox was an infectious disease caused by one of two virus variants, Variola major and Variola minor. The disease was unique to humans and was one of the deadliest diseases in human history. It was declared eradicated worldwide in 1980 following a successful global immunization campaign.

History

Smallpox is believed to have emerged in human populations around 10,000 BCE. The earliest physical evidence of smallpox was found in Egyptian mummies from the 3rd century BCE. The disease played a significant role in human history, affecting populations across continents and contributing to the fall of several civilizations.

Symptoms

Initial symptoms of smallpox included fever, vomiting, and headache. These were followed by characteristic skin lesions that would progress to raised fluid-filled blisters. The disease had a mortality rate of approximately 30% in unvaccinated individuals.

Eradication

The World Health Organization (WHO) launched an intensified global eradication program in 1967. On May 8, 1980, the World Health Assembly declared smallpox eradicated, making it the first disease to be eliminated globally through human intervention.

Introduction

AllePedia is a collaborative online encyclopedia that aims to provide accurate and comprehensive information on a wide range of topics. Working in conjunction with Alleyfind, our specialized search engine, we create an integrated knowledge ecosystem that makes information discovery and learning more accessible than ever.

Features

AllePedia offers:

  • User-friendly interface for easy navigation
  • Comprehensive articles on various topics
  • Community-driven content creation and editing
  • Integration with Alleyfind search engine
  • Regular updates and fact-checking

Getting Started

To begin using AllePedia, simply browse through our articles using the navigation sidebar or search for specific topics using the search function. New users can create an account to contribute content and participate in discussions.

Using Alleyfind

Alleyfind is our integrated search engine that helps you find information both within AllePedia and across the web. It features:

  • Advanced search algorithms
  • Personalized search results
  • Integration with AllePedia articles
  • Real-time search suggestions
